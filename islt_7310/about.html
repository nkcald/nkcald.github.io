Doris Rogers

From MLIS student to librarian

I am Doris Rogers and six years ago, I started working as a part-time substitute clerk in a small community library. Since that time, I have worked in public service, cataloging, and have written several successful library technology grants.

I have a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice Administration and a Master of Public Administration. I am currently pursuing my Master of Library and Information Science (MLIS) degree, without specialization, however, I plan to continue to work in the public library sector. I will complete all requirements for graduation in December 2021.

Prior to going to work at the library, I worked as a federal grants manager, training coordinator and in a variety of administrative positions. I am also a military veteran.

An unknown author once wrote: Proceed as if success is inevitable.
